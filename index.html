William Seymour

Privacy and Security Researcher @ KCL

The modern connected home can be a busy and confusing space. My research seeks to understand people's concerns about smart and connected devices in the home, both for themselves and their families. This insight can be used to design and develop artefacts that allow people to take control of their privacy and autonomy, pushing back unwanted encroachment of the digital on everyday life.

Current Project - Secure AI Assistants

Until the end of 2023 I will be a research associate on the EPSRC-funded Secure AI Assistants (SAIS) project at King's College London. The project is exploring the attack surface of the AI assistant ecosystem in order to specify, verify, and explain secure behaviours.

Past Project - Informing the Future of Smart Homes

I was part of the ICO funded Future of Smart Homes project at the University of Oxford, Seeking to explore the future of data protection in smart and connected homes. The project included a six month ethnographic study of smart device adoption and the deployment of a privacy-empowering technology probe.
